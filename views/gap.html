# Post Harvest Details Description in English Description in Hausa
1 Cleaning Proper hygiene should be maintained by all tomato producers and handlers, not only to reduce postharvest diseases, but also incidence of food-borne illnesses that can be transmitted to consumers.
3 Packaging The wooden/rubber crate and the smooth woven palm basket are recommended packaging materials that should be used for packaging tomatoes with minimum damage. Other Tomato packaging materials are: woven palm baskets that is well woven without rough edges, wooden crates, cardboard boxes, plastic crates, nylon sacks, jute sacks, and polythene bags.
4 Storage For short-term storage (up to a week), tomato fruits can be stored at ambient conditions if there is enough ventilation to reduce the accumulation of heat from respiration. For longer-term storage, ripe tomatoes can be stored at temperatures of about 10–15°C. Harvested fruit should be stored in well-ventilated areas, or in a low oxygen or high carbon dioxide atmosphere. The fruit should never be exposed to temperatures below 12.5°C or chilling injury may result.

2 Fertilizer Application "Use 200–250 kg per hectare of nitrogen (440 to 550 kg Granular Urea), 100 kg of potassium per hectare and 40 to 60 kg of phosphorus per hectare for Tomato cultivation. Apply decomposed organic manure at a rate of 120–150 kg/ha to suppliment soil nutrients."
3 Planting Technique "Transplanting should be done during late afternoon. Seedlings are placed on side of the beds between 21-28 days from seeding. The seedbeds should be irrigated after sowing and they should be done regularly until the seedlings reach a height of 5 to 7 cm. "
4 CPP "Insecticides = Ampligo, Malathion, Carbaryl, Endosulfan, Dichlorophos, Chlorophyriphos and pyrethroids. Fungicide = Dithane Z-78, Dithane M-45, Difolatan, Daconil 2787, Agrocit 50 SD and Chinoinfundozol 50 WP, Difolatan and copper Oxychloride 50, Methionine, Fytolan and Captan. 5 Pest and Disease Control "Tuta absoluta: Best results for managing this pest are achieved by integrated pest management: chemical control [Chlorantraniliprole (Trade name: Rynaxypyr) from Dupont], the use of pheromone traps and mass trapping (Tuta trap tray), cultural control, biological control, resistant varieties and bio-pesticides. Tomato fruit worm: Best results for managing this pest are achieved by alternate sprays with Malathion and Carbaryl at 10-day intervals starting from leaf perforation. Epilachna beetles: Spray with Endosulfan or Dichlorophos or Chlorophyriphos once at 0.1%. Jassids: Use of any contact insecticides like Malathion 0.05% effectively control this pest.
# Production Details Description in English Description in Hausa
1 Land Selection Choose well-drained, fairly light fertile loamy soil with a good moisture holding capacity for growing Tomato.
2 Land Preparation Plough soil to a depth of 30–50cmto ensure good drainage and air content to aid good root establishment. Harrow once after ploughing and apply decomposed organic manure to improve the soil fertility. Properly irrigate and ridge into beds ready for transplant. Apply pre-emergence herbicide, and transplant after 1 or 2 days.
3 Planting Technique Transplanting should be done during late afternoon. Seedlings are placed on side of the beds between 21-28 days from seeding. The seedbeds should be irrigated after sowing and they should be done regularly until the seedlings reach a height of 5 to 7 cm.
4 Harvesting Tomato can be harvested at any of the following stages: Immature: Harvest before the seeds fully developed and before the jelly-like substances surrounding the seeds are formed. Mature green: The fully-grown fruit with a brownish ring at stem-scar. Hard ripe: Nearly all red or pink with firm flesh Over ripe: Fully colored and soft. Harvest tomato by holding the fruit firmly, but gently, and pull from the plant by holding the stem with one hand and the fruit with the other, breaking the stalk just above the calyx that has formed to protect the bud.
5 Mechanization Smallholder farmers in the Tomato value chain require access to machinery and equipment for cultivation: • Land preparation: Tractor, Plough, Tillage equipment, Harrower, Ridger • Storage: Refrigerator .

2 Drying Sun-dry sorghum after harvest either in the field or off-field for proper drying upto about 1-2 weeks to reduce moisture content to about 12%, depending on the weather condition after harvest.
3 Packaging After proper drying of sorghum has been achieved, it should be properly bagged for storage in new bags that are free of contaminants. Stored in 50−100kg bags made from either jute, cotton polypropylene or multi-layer paper.
4 Storage "Sorghum grains should be stored in bags, with a Phostoxin tablet/100kg seeds against pest at 12% moisture content. Store/warehouses should be sited in flood-free, well-drained area with good access roads. The walls should be plastered with cement and the roof covered with corrugated iron sheets. Doors and windows should be well-fitted. . In-coming grains should be well graded for moisture content, pest infestation level, contaminants, etc." .

1 Weed Control A suitable pre-emergence herbicide (like pendimethalin, primextra gold) should be sprayed after the sowing of the sorghum field. Few weeks after emergence of sorghum, a selective post emergence herbicide is recommended for use or weeding done a minimum of 1-2 times at intervals of 4 weeks before harvest.
2 Fertilizer Application "Farm Yard Manure should be applied at 2-3t/ha during land preparation. And NPK 15:15:15 fertilizers (4 bags 200 kg/ha) be applied at planting and Urea applied at the rate of 2 bags (100 kg/ha) applied prior to flowerings. Use 2 cork stoppers full of NPK 15:15:15 buried in a 5cm deep hole 5 to 10 days after emergence with 1 cork stopper full of Urea 4 to 5 weeks after sowing."
3 Planting Technique Sorghum can be planted using animal-drawn planters or dibbler. Plant at a depth of 2.5cm in areas with sufficient water and maximum of 5cm in drier areas at 75cm X 25-30cm. Planting is done around May to June.
4 CPP "Farmers should be well-kitted at all times with the following protective coverings before using CPPs: • Hand gloves reaching elbow level to protect their hands from chemical contamination. • Face mask to prevent inhaling chemical substances in gaseous state • Overalls to the knee level to prevent splashing of CPPs on the body during spraying • Rubber boats reaching the knee level will prevent CPPs splashing on the feet"

# Production Details Description in English Description in Hausa
1 Land Selection A fertile well drained clay loam soils with high moisture retention capacity is best for growing sorghum.
2 Land Preparation Use conventional tillage by ploughing and harrowing if tractor is available before ridge making at a row spacing of 75cm, to allow for deeper roots penetration and minnimize the problems of weeds.
3 Planting Technique Sorghum can be planted using animal-drawn planters or dibbler. Plant at a depth of 2.5cm in areas with sufficient water and maximum of 5cm in drier areas at 75cm X 25-30cm. Planting is done around May to June.
4 Harvesting Harvest grains early, using manual or mechanical means. Panicles should be cut from the standing stalks either manually or mechanically when moisture content is about 16-20%.
5 Mechanization "Smallholder farmers growing sorghum requires access to machinery and equipment for cultivation such as tractor moulted with harrower, plough and ridger. Others like Motorized weeder and Knapsack sprayers for spraying herbicides.
